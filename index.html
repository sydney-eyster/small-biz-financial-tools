Parts Suppliers & Local Services
Vetted national suppliers for self-serve car wash components, plus KC-area service categories. Always get 2–3 quotes for major repairs.
National Parts & Equipment Suppliers
Kleen-Rite Corp
kleen-rite.com — The largest self-serve car wash supplier in the US. Pumps, guns, nozzles, coin meters, bay equipment, chemicals. Ships nationwide.
National Carwash Solutions (NCS)
carwashsupply.com — Major distributor for equipment parts, soap/chemical supplies, and kiosk components. Has a service network.
Dultmeier Sales
dultmeier.com — Omaha-based (close to KC) industrial pump, hose, and pressure wash equipment supplier. Strong on Comet and AR pump parts.
Ryko / PDQ Manufacturing
ryko.com — OEM parts and service for Ryko-brand self-serve and automatic equipment. If your bays are Ryko, start here for parts.
Water Systems Co.
watersystemsco.com — Water reclaim systems, softeners, RO units, and treatment. Critical for a 20-yr-old facility likely running hard KC water.
Grainger Industrial
grainger.com — For electrical components, motors, drives, bearings, and fasteners. Same-day pickup at KC branch (9200 Marshall Dr, Lenexa).
KC-Area Trade Services
Plumbing
For pipe repairs, valve replacements, and freeze damage. Search commercial plumbers KC — look for ones with industrial or car wash experience. Get a service contract if possible.
Electrical
Panel work, motor wiring, kiosk circuits. Needs to be a licensed commercial electrician. Search commercial electricians KC.
Pressure Wash Equipment Repair
Search local repair shops — Comet and AR pump specialists are most useful for self-serve bays. Some Kleen-Rite distributors also do field service.
HVAC / Boiler (Freeze Prevention)
Critical for KC winters. Your bay heating system needs annual service before November. Search commercial HVAC KC.
Concrete & Structural
Bay floor cracking, drain repair, and wall patching are common at 20 years. Look for contractors with commercial/industrial concrete experience. Search here.
Car Wash Industry Consultants
International Carwash Association (ICA) — member directory includes regional consultants, equipment dealers, and service providers. Useful for finding KC-area specialists.
Tips for a 20-year-old facility: At this age, proactive service contracts with a plumber and electrician are worth it — you'll call them anyway, and having an existing relationship means faster response. Kleen-Rite is the default starting point for most self-serve parts orders; their catalog covers 90% of common wear items. For KC-specific freeze damage (a real risk), ensure your bay heat and pipe insulation is inspected every fall before the first hard freeze.

Maintenance — 20-Year-Old Facility
A 20-yr-old self-serve car wash typically needs $18,000–$28,000/year in maintenance. High-wear items: pump rebuilds ($2–4k), bay hardware ($1–3k/bay), electrical ($2–5k), plumbing ($1–3k), structural ($1–2k). Spring sees a spike from winter freeze damage.
Spread monthly with a spring spike. ~60% routine, ~40% reserve for unplanned repairs.
Acquisition & Financing
Models the purchase price and debt service. SDE (Seller's Discretionary Earnings) = profit + owner's add-backs. Car washes typically trade at 3–5× SDE.
4× is typical for a KC-area self-serve in good condition. 3× = distressed / motivated seller. 5–6× = strong location, clean books.
One-time or personal expenses run through the business (owner vehicle, travel, personal phone, etc.) added back to get true SDE. Common range: $5k–$20k.
Purchase price will calculate from SDE × multiple above.
